Universities

This page is built like a real decision page. Instead of throwing names at the wall, it explains what each university is known for, where it is located, and what kind of student might fit there.

🔎
Engineering

Technical University of Munich

Strong for technical subjects, research culture, and students who want a highly structured academic environment.

  • Best for STEM-heavy profiles
  • Large university-city energy
  • Competitive, so plan carefully
Technology

RWTH Aachen University

A classic pick for engineering-minded students who want strong technical reputation and a student city feel.

  • Known for engineering
  • Aachen is a student town
  • Good fit for technical focus
Research

Heidelberg University

Often associated with research depth, academic tradition, and a strong reputation across many disciplines.

  • Historic academic environment
  • Good for research-oriented students
  • Classic university atmosphere
Broad Range

LMU Munich

A major university with a broad academic profile, attractive for students who want strong overall reputation.

  • Wide subject coverage
  • Munich is dynamic but expensive
  • Great for serious academic planning
City Campus

Technical University of Berlin

A fit for students who want a mix of technical study and the big-city rhythm of Berlin.

  • Urban atmosphere
  • Technical subject strength
  • Good for independent students
Urban

University of Hamburg

A major university in a vibrant port city with a broad academic culture and a large student population.

  • Big-city student life
  • Wide academic environment
  • Useful for many fields

How to choose a university properly

Do not pick only by reputation. Compare subject fit, city costs, language requirements, campus culture, and whether the program actually supports your career direction. A less famous university can still be a better fit.
